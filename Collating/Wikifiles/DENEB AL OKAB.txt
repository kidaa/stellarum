'''Zeta Aquilae''' (ζ Aql, ζ Aquilae) is a [[double star]] in the equatorial [[constellation]] of [[Aquila (constellation)|Aquila]]. It has the traditional name '''Deneb el Okab''', meaning "the tail of the falcon". As a third magnitude star,<ref name=sps1966/> Zeta Aquilae is readily visible with the naked eye. [[Parallax]] measurements place it at a distance of approximately {{Convert|83|ly|pc|abbr=off|lk=on}} from [[Earth]].<ref name=aaa474_2_653/>

==Properties==
Zeta Aquilae has a [[stellar classification]] of A0&nbsp;Vn,<ref name=aj74_375/> with the [[luminosity class]] 'V' indicating is a [[main sequence]] star that is generating energy through the [[nuclear fusion]] of hydrogen at its core. It has more than double the mass and twice the radius of the [[Sun]],<ref name=aaa487_3_1041/><ref name=apj698_2_1068/> and is radiating more than 39 times the Sun's luminosity.<ref name=apj698_2_1068/> The [[effective temperature]] of the star's outer envelope is about 9620&nbsp;K,<ref name=apj698_2_1068/> which gives it the white hue typical of A-type stars. The estimated age of this star is 50–150 million years.<ref name=aaa487_3_1041/>

This star is rotating rapidly, with a [[stellar rotation|projected rotational velocity]] of 317&nbsp;km s<sup>−1</sup> giving a lower bound on the [[azimuth]]al velocity along the equator.<ref name=aaa463_2_671/> As a result, it has a pronounced equatorial bulge, causing the star to assume an [[oblate spheroid]]al shape. The equatorial radius is about 30.7% greater than the polar radius.<ref name=aaa487_3_1041/> Because of the [[Doppler effect]], this rapid rotation makes the [[absorption line]]s in the star's [[spectrum]] broaden and smear out, as indicated by the 'n' suffix in the stellar class.

Astronomers use Zeta Aquilae as a telluric standard star.<ref name=mnras314_2_420/> That is, the spectrum of this star is used to correct for [[telluric contamination]] from the Earth's atmosphere when examining the spectra of neighboring stars.<ref name=niessen2007/> Observation of this star in the [[infrared]] band during the [[2MASS]] survey appeared to reveal [[infrared excess|excess emission]]. However, the distribution of this emission couldn't be readily explained by a conjectured [[debris disk|disk of circumstellar dust]].<ref name=aaa487_3_1041/> Instead, the detection was later ascribed to errors caused by saturation of the near-infrared detectors.<ref name=apj698_2_1068/>

This star has two 12th magnitude companions at [[angular separation]]s of 6.5 and 158.6 [[arcsecond]]s.{{citation needed|date=January 2012}}

==Etymology==
It has the traditional names '''Deneb el Okab''', from an [[Arabic language|Arabic]] term ذنب العقاب ''ðanab al-<sup>c</sup>uqāb'' meaning "the tail of the falcon", and the [[Standard Mandarin|Mandarin]] names '''Woo''' and '''Yuë''', derived from and represent the state  '''[[Wu (state)|Wu]]''' (吳),  an old state was located at the mouth of the [[Yangtze River]], and  '''[[Yue (state)|Yue]]''' (越), an old state in [[Zhejiang]] province<ref>{{zh icon}} [http://www.lcsd.gov.hk/CE/Museum/Space/Research/StarName/e_research_chinengstarzone_b.htm#HeavenlyMarketEnclosure English-Chinese Glossary of Chinese Star Regions, Asterisms and Star Name], Hong Kong Space Museum. Accessed on line November 23, 2010.</ref> (together with [[19 Capricorni]] or [[Psi Capricorni|ψ Capricorni]] in R.H.Allen's version,<ref>[http://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Gazetteer/Topics/astronomy/_Texts/secondary/ALLSTA/Capricornus*.html Star Names - R.H.Allen p.142]</ref> in ''[[Girl (Chinese constellation)|Twelve States]]'' asterism).  According to the R.H. Allen's works, it shares names with [[Epsilon Aquilae|ε Aquilae]].<ref>[http://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Gazetteer/Topics/astronomy/_Texts/secondary/ALLSTA/Aquila*.html Star Names - R.H.Allen p.61]</ref>

In [[Chinese language|Chinese]], {{lang|zh|天市左垣}} ({{lang|zh-Latn|Tiān Shì Zuǒ Yuán}}), meaning ''[[Heavenly Market enclosure|Left Wall of Heavenly Market Enclosure]]'', refers to an asterism which is represent eleven old states in China which is marking the left borderline of the enclosure, consisting of ζ Aquilae, [[Delta Herculis|δ Herculis]], [[Lambda Herculis|λ Herculis]], [[Mu Herculis|μ Herculis]], [[Omicron Herculis|ο Herculis]], [[112 Herculis]], [[Theta1 Serpentis|θ<sup>1</sup> Serpentis]], [[Eta Serpentis|η Serpentis]], [[Nu Ophiuchi|ν Ophiuchi]], [[Xi Serpentis|ξ Serpentis]] and [[Eta Ophiuchi|η Ophiuchi]].<ref>{{zh icon}} ''中國星座神話'', written by 陳久金. Published by 台灣書房出版有限公司, 2005, ISBN 978-986-7332-25-7.</ref> Consequently, ζ Aquilae itself is known as {{lang|zh|天市左垣六}} ({{lang|zh-Latn|Tiān Shì Zuǒ Yuán liù}}, {{lang-en|the Sixth Star of Left Wall of Heavenly Market Enclosure}}), represent the states which have mentioned above.<ref>{{zh icon}} [http://www.lcsd.gov.hk/CE/Museum/Space/Research/StarName/c_research_chinengstars_c_d.htm 香港太空館 - 研究資源 - 亮星中英對照表], Hong Kong Space Museum. Accessed on line November 23, 2010.</ref>

In the catalogue of stars in the ''Calendarium of Al Achsasi Al Mouakket'', this star was designated '''Dzeneb al Tair''' (from '''ذنب الطائر''' - ''ðanab aṭ-ṭā’ir''), which was translated into [[Latin]] as '''Cauda (Vulturis) Volantis''', meaning ''the eagle's tail''.<ref>{{cite journal
