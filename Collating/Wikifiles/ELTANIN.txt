'''Gamma Draconis''' (γ Dra, γ Draconis) is the [[Bayer designation]] for a [[star]] in the northern [[constellation]] of [[Draco (constellation)|Draco]]. It has the traditional name '''Etamin''' or '''Eltanin''' and the Flamsteed designation '''33 Draconis'''.<ref name="SIMBAD"/><ref name="KALER"/><ref name="WIKISKY"/> Despite its gamma designation, it is actually the brightest star in Draco at magnitude 2.4,<ref name="LEEUWEN"/> outshining Rastaban ([[Beta Draconis]]) by nearly half a magnitude. Its proximity to the [[zenith|zenith point]] directly overhead of [[London]] has earned it the name "Zenith Star." As for other places, it is relatively easy to locate in the night sky. If one finds [[Alpha Lyrae|Vega]], Eltanin is the red star just north-northwest of it.

Eltanin lies around {{Convert|154.3|ly|pc|abbr=off|lk=on}} away,<ref name="LEEUWEN"/> as determined by [[parallax]] measurements from the [[Hipparcos]] astrometry satellite.<ref name=aaa323_L49/><ref name=GSM/> In 1728, while unsuccessfully attempting to measure the [[parallax]] of this star, [[James Bradley]] discovered the [[aberration of light]] resulting from the movement of the Earth. Bradley's discovery confirmed [[Copernicus]]' [[Heliocentrism|theory]] that the Earth revolved around the Sun.<ref name="KALER"/>

==Properties==
Gamma Draconis is an [[stellar evolution|evolved]] [[giant star]] with a [[stellar classification]] of K5&nbsp;III.<ref name=araa11_29/> Since 1943, the [[spectrum]] of this star has served as one of the stable anchor points by which other stars are classified.<ref name=baas25_1319/> It has 72%<ref name=aaa533_A107/> more mass than the Sun and it has expanded to around 48<ref name=aaa526_A100/> times the Sun's girth. It is radiating about 471<ref name=aaa526_A100/> times as much luminosity as the Sun from its [[stellar atmosphere|outer atmosphere]] at an [[effective temperature]] of 3,930&nbsp;K.<ref name=aaa526_A100/> This is cooler than the Sun, giving this star the orange-hued glow of a [[K-type star]].<ref name=csiro/>

Eltanin has a faint 13.4 magnitude optical companion that may be a physical companion. If so, the two are separated by about 1000&nbsp;[[Astronomical Unit|AU]]. The luminosity of this object suggests it is a [[red dwarf]] star.<ref name=apj651_2_1126/> In 1.5 million years, Eltanin will pass within 28 light years of Earth. At this point (assuming its current absolute magnitude does not change) it will be the brightest star in the night sky, nearly as bright as [[Sirius]] is at present.<ref name="KALER"/>

==In culture==
The name Etamin comes from the [[Arabic language|Arabic]] التنين At-Tinnin ''The great serpent''. The name "Rastaban" was formerly used for Eltanin, and the two terms share an [[Arabic language|Arabic]] root meaning "serpent" or "dragon." This star, along with [[Beta Draconis|β Dra]] (Rastaban), [[Mu Draconis|μ Dra]] (Erakis), [[Nu Draconis|ν Dra]] (Kuma) and [[Xi Draconis|ξ Dra]] (Grumium) were '''Al ʽAwāïd''', "the Mother Camels", which was later known as the '''Quinque Dromedarii'''.<ref name=allen1963/>

In [[Chinese language|Chinese]], {{lang|zh|天棓}} ({{lang|zh-Latn|Tiān Bàng}}), meaning ''[[Purple Forbidden enclosure|Celestial Flail]]'', refers to an asterism consisting of γ Draconis, [[Xi Draconis|ξ Draconis]], [[Nu Draconis|ν Draconis]], [[Beta Draconis|β Draconis]] and [[Iota Herculis|ι Herculis]].<ref>{{zh icon}} ''中國星座神話'', written by 陳久金. Published by 台灣書房出版有限公司, 2005, ISBN 978-986-7332-25-7.</ref> Consequently, γ Draconis itself is known as {{lang|zh|天棓四}} ({{lang|zh-Latn|Tiān Bàng sì}}, {{lang-en|the Fourth Star of Celestial Flail}}.)<ref>{{zh icon}} [http://www.lcsd.gov.hk/CE/Museum/Space/Research/StarName/c_research_chinengstars_e_g.htm 香港太空館 - 研究資源 - 亮星中英對照表], Hong Kong Space Museum. Accessed on line November 23, 2010.</ref>

[[USS Etamin (AK-93)]] was a [[United States Navy]] [[Crater class cargo ship]] named after the star.
