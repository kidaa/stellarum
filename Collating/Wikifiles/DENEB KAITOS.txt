'''Beta Ceti''' (β Cet, β Ceti) is the brightest [[star]] in the [[constellation]] [[Cetus]]. Although it has the [[Bayer designation]] "beta", it is actually brighter than [[Alpha Ceti]]. It has the traditional names '''Deneb Kaitos''' and '''Diphda'''.  This orange giant is easy to identify due to its location in an otherwise dark section of the [[celestial sphere]]. Based on [[parallax]] measurements, it lies at an estimated distance of {{Convert|96.3|ly|pc|abbr=off|lk=on}} from Earth.<ref name=aaa474_2_653/>

== Properties ==
[[File:Bceti xray.jpg|thumb|left|200px|Image of orange [[giant star|giant]] Beta Ceti from [[NASA]]'s [[Chandra X-ray Observatory]].]]
Deneb Kaitos has an [[apparent visual magnitude]] of 2.02,<ref name=clpl4_99/> making it the [[List of stars in Cetus|brightest star in Cetus]]. The [[stellar classification]] of this star is K0&nbsp;III,<ref name=mnras328_1_45/> although some sources list a classification of G9.5&nbsp;III<ref name=sagesser_jordan2005/> indicating that it lies along the dividing line separating G-type from K-type stars. The luminosity class of 'III' means that it is a [[giant star]] that has consumed the hydrogen at its core and [[stellar evolution|evolved]] away from an A-type [[main sequence]] star.<ref name=sagesser_jordan2005/> After passing through the [[red giant]] stage, it underwent the [[helium flash]] event and is presently generating energy through the [[thermonuclear fusion]] of [[helium]] at its core.<ref name=apj562_1_L83/> Beta Ceti will remain in this mode for over 100 million years.<ref name=chandra/>

The [[effective temperature]] of the star's outer envelope is about 4,797&nbsp;K,<ref name=aj135_1_209/> giving it the characteristic orange hue of a K-type star.<ref name=csiro/> In spite of its cooler temperature, Deneb Kaitos is much brighter than the Sun with a [[bolometer|bolometric]] luminosity of about 145 times the [[Solar luminosity|luminosity of the Sun]],<ref name="Kaler"/> resulting from a radius 18<ref name=aaa535_A59/> times as large as the Sun and a mass that is 2.8 times the [[Solar mass|Sun's mass]].<ref name=sagesser_jordan2005/>

This star displays flaring activity that results in random outbursts that increase the luminosity of the star over intervals lasting several days. This is a much longer duration than for comparable solar flare activity on the Sun, which typically last for periods measured in hours.<ref name=apj562_1_L83/> In 2005, a relatively high rate of X-ray emission was detected with the [[XMM-Newton]] space observatory.<ref name=sagesser_jordan2005/> It is emitting about 2,000 times the X-ray luminosity of the Sun, allowing the star to be imaged with the Chandra X-ray Observatory.<ref name=chandra/>

== Etymology ==
The traditional name Deneb Kaitos is Arabic '''ألضنب ألقيتوس ألجنوب''' - ''Al Dhanab al Ḳaiṭos al Janūbīyy'' for "southern tail of Cetus"; it is also known as Diphda, "frog", from the Arabic '''الضفدع الثاني''' ''aḍ-ḍafdaʿ aṯ-ṯānī'' "the second frog" ("the first frog" is [[Fomalhaut]]).<ref name=allen1963/>

In [[Chinese astronomy]], Deneb Kaitos is called 土司空, [[Pinyin]]: Tǔsīkōng, meaning ''Master of Constructions'', because this star is marking itself and stand alone in ''Master of Constructions'' asterism, [[Legs (Chinese constellation)|Legs]] mansion (see : [[Chinese constellation]]).<ref>{{zh icon}} [http://aeea.nmns.edu.tw/2006/0607/ap060709.html AEEA (Activities of Exhibition and Education in Astronomy) 天文教育資訊網 2006 年 7 月 9 日]</ref> 土司空 (Tǔsīkōng), westernized into '''Too Sze Kung''' by R.H. Allen and the meaning is "Superintendent of Earthworks." <ref>[http://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Gazetteer/Topics/astronomy/_Texts/secondary/ALLSTA/Cetus*.html Star Name - R.H. Allen p. 160]</ref>

==In culture==
[[USS Diphda (AKA-59)]] is once of [[United States]] navy ship.
