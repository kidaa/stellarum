'''Mu Draconis''' (μ&nbsp;Draconis, μ&nbsp;Dra) is a [[binary star]] with a combined [[magnitude (astronomy)|magnitude]] of 4.92<sup>m</sup> located approximately 85&nbsp;[[light year]]s from the [[Solar System]], near the head of the [[constellation]] [[Draco (constellation)|Draco]]. The component stars are nearly identical yellow-white stars in a tight 672&nbsp;year orbit. Each is of the [[stellar classification|spectral class]]&nbsp;'''F7V''' and has a [[visual magnitude]] of 5.8<sup>m</sup>. Under ideal seeing conditions, the pair can be resolved with a small {{Convert|60|mm|in|abbr=on}} aperture [[telescope]] at about 120&nbsp;power.

== Cultural references ==
[[Science fiction]] writer [[Frank Herbert]] chose [[Arrakis]] as the name of the primary planet in his famous [[Dune series|''Dune'' series]] of novels, aware that the word "Arrakis" is the transliteration into English of the Arabic words for "the dancer" (al-Raqis).{{Citation needed|date=March 2010}} However, Herbert uses the name not to describe μ Draconis, but the fictional ''planet'' Arrakis as the third planet of the star [[Canopus|Canopus (α&nbsp;Carinae)]] in the constellation Carina.
