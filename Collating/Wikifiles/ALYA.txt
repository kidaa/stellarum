'''Theta Serpentis''' (θ Ser, θ Serpentis) is a [[star system]] in the [[constellation]] [[Serpens]].  It is approximately 132 [[light year]]s from Earth.  Theta Serpentis also has the traditional name '''Alya''', or '''Alga''', from the [[Arabic language|Arabic]] الية ''’alyah'' "[[fat-tailed sheep|fat tail]] (of a sheep)".

In the catalogue of stars in the ''Calendarium'' of [[Al Achsasi al Mouakket]], this star was designated '''Dzaneb al Haiyet''', which was translated into [[Latin]] as '''Cauda Serpentis''', meaning ''the serpent's tail''.<ref>{{cite journal

Accessed on line November 23, 2010.</ref>

Both '''Theta-1 Serpentis''' and '''Theta-2 Serpentis''' are white [[stellar classification|A-type]] [[main sequence|main sequence dwarf]]s.  θ¹ has an [[apparent magnitude]] of +4.62 while the slightly dimmer θ² has a [[apparent magnitude|magnitude]] of +4.98.  These two stars are 22 [[arcsecond]]s apart on the sky, putting them at least 900 [[astronomical unit]]s apart with an orbital period of at least 14,000 years. Both stars are similar to each other in all respects, having [[luminosity|luminosities]] of 18 and 13 times solar respectively, radii of about twice solar and also [[solar mass|masses]] of roughly 2 times that of our Sun. Both star have a surface temperature of 8,000 [[kelvin]]s.

The third star in the system, '''Theta Serpentis C''', is a yellow G-type star with an apparent magnitude of +6.71.  It is separated by 7 [[arcminute]]s from θ².
