'''Gamma Canis Majoris''' (γ CMa, γ Canis Majoris) is a [[star]] in the [[constellation]] [[Canis Major]].  It has the traditional name '''Muliphein''',<ref>[http://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Gazetteer/Topics/astronomy/_Texts/secondary/ALLSTA/Canis_Major*.html Star Name - R.H.Allen p.130]</ref> not to be confused with ''Muhlifain'', which is [[Gamma Centauri]]; both names derive from the same Arabic root, محلفين ''muħlifayn''.

It is unclear exactly why this relatively faint star was given the "gamma" designation, but it is possibly because it is in the same part of the constellation as Sirius (alpha) and Murzim (beta).

γ Canis Majoris is a blue-white [[stellar classification|B-type]] [[bright giant]] with an [[apparent magnitude]] of +4.11. It is approximately 402 [[light years]] from [[Earth]]. This star has 5.6 times the radius of the Sun and the outer atmosphere has an [[effective temperature]] of 13,596&nbsp;K.<ref name=mnras189_601/>
