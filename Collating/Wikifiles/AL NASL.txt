'''Gamma Sagittarii''' ('''Gamma&nbsp;Sgr''', '''γ&nbsp;Sagittarii''', '''γ&nbsp;Sgr''') is a 3rd-[[Magnitude (astronomy)|magnitude]] [[star]] in the [[zodiac]] [[constellation]] [[Sagittarius (constellation)|Sagittarius]]. The location of this star forms the tip of the arrow in the bow of Sagittarius the [[Centaur]]. It has the proper names '''Alnasl''' (''Nasl'', ''El Nasl''), '''Nushaba''' (''Nash'') and '''Warida'''.<ref name=olcott1907/> 

This star is close enough to the [[Earth]] that its distance can be determined using [[parallax]], yielding a value of about {{Convert|96.9|ly|pc|abbr=off|lk=on}}. It has an [[apparent visual magnitude]] of +2.98,<ref name=scfs/> making it the [[List of stars in Sagittarius|seventh brightest star]] in the constellation. A [[stellar classification]] of K1&nbsp;III reveals that this is a [[giant star]],<ref name=scfs/> having expanded to an estimated 12 times the [[solar radius|Sun's radius]].<ref name=CADARS/> This means it has exhausted the hydrogen in its core and [[stellar evolution|evolved]] away from the [[main sequence]]. The abundance of elements other than hydrogen and helium in this star, what astronomers term the star's [[metallicity]],<ref name=ajss74_1075/> is lower than in the [[Sun]]. Gamma Sagittarii has an [[effective temperature]] of 4,760&nbsp;K,<ref name=ajss74_1075/> compared to 5,778&nbsp;K for the Sun.<ref name=csiro/> It is this lower temperature that gives Gamma Sagittarii the orange hue that is a characteristic of [[K-type star]]s.

There is a fainter optical companion located about 50&nbsp;[[arcminute]]s north of this star. It is magnitude 4.7 [[Cepheid variable]] star designated [[W Sagittarii]].<ref name=kaler/><ref name=burnham1978/>

==Name and etymology==
*Alnasl is derived from the Arabic النصل ''al-naşl'' meaning "arrowhead".,<ref name=ridpath1989/> Nushaba is derived from the Arabic ''Zujj al-Nashshaba'' meaning the same as Alnasl.,<ref name=allen1899/> Warida is derived from  النعم الوارد ''Al Naʽām al Wārid'', the Going Ostriches, the asterism consisting this star, [[Delta Sagittarii|δ Sgr]], [[Epsilon Sagittarii|ε Sgr]] and [[Eta Sagittarii|η Sgr]].<ref name=allen1899>{{cite book

*In [[Chinese language|Chinese]], {{lang|zh|箕}} ({{lang|zh-Latn|Jī}}), meaning ''[[Winnowing Basket (Chinese constellation)|Winnowing Basket]]'', refers to an asterism consisting of γ Sagittarii, [[Delta Sagittarii|δ Sagittarii]], [[Epsilon Sagittarii|ε Sagittarii]] and [[Eta Sagittarii|η Sagittarii]].
