'''Alpha Trianguli Australis''' (α TrA, α Trianguli Australis) is the brightest [[star]] in the southern [[circumpolar constellation]] [[Triangulum Australe]], forming an apex of a triangle with [[Beta Trianguli Australis|Beta]] and [[Gamma Trianguli Australis]] that gives the constellation its name ([[Latin]] for southern triangle). This star has the traditional name '''Atria''', which is merely a contraction of its [[Bayer designation]].<ref name=ridpath1989/> In traditional Chinese it is called 三角形三 ([[Standard Chinese|Mandarin]]: sān jiǎo xín sān), the Third Star of the Triangle.

Alpha Trianguli Australis is a [[bright giant]] star with an [[apparent magnitude]] of +1.91. Based upon [[parallax]] measurements, this star is located roughly {{Convert|391|ly|pc|abbr=off|lk=on}} distant from the Earth.<ref name=aaa474_2_653/> The estimated age of the star is 48 million years old; sufficiently old for a massive star to evolve away from the [[main sequence]] and expand into a giant.<ref name=aaa120_1_21/> It has a mass roughly seven times the [[solar mass|mass of the Sun]], but is emitting about 5,500 times the [[solar luminosity|Sun's luminosity]]. The [[effective temperature]] of the star's outer envelope is 4,150&nbsp;K,<ref name=aaa120_1_21/> which gives it the characteristic orange hue of a K-type star.<ref name=csiro/> With a diameter 130 times that of our sun, it would almost reach the orbit of Venus if placed at the centre of our solar system. The proper name Atria is a contraction of its Bayer designation.<ref name="kaleratria">{{cite web|url=http://stars.astro.illinois.edu/sow/atria.html|title=Atria (Alpha Trianguli Australis |last=Kaler|first=Jim|work=Stars|publisher=University of Illinois|accessdate=17 October 2012}}</ref>

There is evidence that Atria may be a [[binary star]]. It displays unusual properties for a star of its class, including [[flare star|stellar flares]] and a higher than normal emission of X-rays. These can be explained by a young, [[Stellar magnetic field|magnetically active]] companion with a [[stellar classification]] of about G0&nbsp;V. Such a star would have a mass similar to the Sun, with an orbital period of at least 130&nbsp;years. Young, [[G-type star]]s have a high temperature [[corona]] and frequently emit flares causing sudden increases in luminosity. The pair may be separated by about 50&nbsp;[[Astronomical Unit]]s.<ref name=apj658_2_L107/>

==In culture==
Atria appears on the [[flag of Brazil]], symbolizing the state of [[Rio Grande do Sul]].<ref name=brazil_flag/>
