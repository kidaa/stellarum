'''Pi Persei''' (π Per) is a [[star]] in the [[constellation]] [[Perseus (constellation)|Perseus]]. It also has the traditional name '''Gorgonea Secunda'''.

Pi Persei has an [[apparent magnitude]] of +4.7 and [[stellar classification|spectral class]] A2V. The star is about 362 [[light year]]s from Earth. 

In [[Chinese astronomy]], Gorgonea Secunda is called 積屍, [[Pinyin]]: Jīshī, meaning ''Heap of Corpses'', because this star is marking itself and stand alone in ''Heap of Corpses'' asterism, [[Stomach (Chinese constellation)|Stomach]] mansion (see : [[Chinese constellation]]).<ref>{{zh icon}}   [http://aeea.nmns.edu.tw/2006/0607/ap060711.html AEEA (Activities of Exhibition and Education in Astronomy) 天文教育資訊網 2006 年 7 月 11 日]</ref> 積屍 (Jīshī), westernized into '''Tseih She''', but the name ''Tseih She'' was designated for [[Beta Persei|β Per]] (Algol) by R.H. Allen and the meaning is "the Piled-up Corpses" <ref>[http://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Gazetteer/Topics/astronomy/_Texts/secondary/ALLSTA/Perseus*.html Richard Hinckley Allen: Star Names — Their Lore and Meaning: Perseus]</ref>
