'''Alpha Persei''' ('''Alpha&nbsp;Per''', '''α&nbsp;Persei''', '''α&nbsp;Per''') is the brightest [[star]] in the northern [[constellation]] of [[Perseus (constellation)|Perseus]], just outshining the constellation's best known star, [[Algol]]. Known by the traditional names '''Mirfak''' and '''Algenib''', it is a [[circumpolar star]] when viewed from the latitude of [[New York City]] or higher.<ref name=allen1899/> It has an [[apparent visual magnitude]] of 1.8,<ref name=aaa485_1_303/> placing it among the [[List of brightest stars|brightest stars]] in the sky. Mirfak lies in the midst of a [[star cluster|cluster of stars]] eponymously named the [[Alpha Persei Cluster]], or ''Melotte 20'', which is easily visible in [[binoculars]] and includes many of the fainter stars in the constellation.<ref name=kaler/> The distance to this star has been determined using the [[parallax]] technique, putting it {{Convert|510|ly|pc|abbr=off|ly=on}} away.<ref name=aaa474_2_653/>

== Properties ==
The [[spectrum]] of Alpha Persei matches a [[stellar classification]] of F5&nbsp;Ib,<ref name=mnras402_2_1369/> revealing it to be a [[supergiant star]] in the latter stages of its [[stellar evolution|evolution]]. It has a similar spectrum to [[Procyon]], though the latter star is much less luminous. This difference is highlighted in their spectral designation under the [[Stellar classification#Yerkes spectral classification|Yerkes spectral classification]], published in 1943, where stars are ranked on luminosity as well as spectral typing. Procyon is thus F5&nbsp;IV,<ref name=ramanamurthy2007/> a [[subgiant star]]. Since 1943, the [[spectrum]] of Alpha Persei has served as one of the stable anchor points by which other stars are classified.<ref name=baas25_1319/>

Mirfak has about 7.3<ref name=mnras402_2_1369/> times the Sun's mass  and has expanded to roughly 60<ref name=aj118_6_3032/> times the size of the Sun. It is radiating 5,000<ref name=kaler/> times as much luminosity as the Sun from its [[stellar atmosphere|outer atmosphere]] at an [[effective temperature]] of 6,350&nbsp;K,<ref name=mnras402_2_1369/> which creates the yellow-white glow of an [[F-type star]]. In the [[Hertzsprung–Russell Diagram]], Mirfak lies inside the region in which [[Cepheid variable]]s are found.<ref name=apj664_2_1093/> It is thus useful in the study of these stars, which are important [[standard candle]]s.<ref name=kaler/>

== Etymology and cultural significance ==
*The names Mirfak and Algenib are [[Arabic language|Arabic]] in origin. The former, meaning 'Elbow' and also written Mirphak, Marfak  or Mirzac, comes from the Arabic ''Mirfaq al-Thurayya'', while Algenib, also spelt Algeneb, Elgenab, Gęnib, Chenib or Alchemb, is derived from الجنب ''al-janb'', or الجانب ''al-jānib'', 'the flank' or 'side'.<ref name=allen1899/><ref name=pa52_3_14/> [[Gamma Pegasi]] also bears the name Algenib.<ref>[http://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Gazetteer/Topics/astronomy/_Texts/secondary/ALLSTA/Pegasus*.html Pegasus]</ref>
*''Hinali'i'' is the name of the star in [[Native Hawaiians|Native Hawaiian]] [[astronomy]]. The name of the star is meant to commemorate a great [[tsunami]] and mark the beginning of the migration of Maui. According to some [[Folklore in Hawaii|Hawaiian folklore]], Hinali'i is the point of separation between the earth and the sky that happened during the creation of the [[Milky Way]].<ref name=malamalama29_2_8/>
*Assemani alluded to a title on the Borgian globe, ''Mughammid'' (مغمد), or ''Muliammir al Thurayya'' (ملىمرٱلطرى), the Concealer of the Pleiades, which, from its location, may be for this star.<ref name=allen1899/>
*This star, together with [[Delta Persei|δ Per]], [[Psi Persei|ψ Per]], [[Sigma Persei|σ Per]], [[Gamma Persei|γ Per]] and [[Eta Persei|η Per]], has been called ''the Segment of Perseus''.<ref name=allen1899>{{cite book

*In [[Chinese language|Chinese]], {{lang|zh|天船}} ({{lang|zh-Latn|Tiān Chuán}}), meaning ''[[Stomach (Chinese constellation)|Celestial Boat]]'', refers to an asterism consisting of α Persei, [[Eta Persei|η Persei]], [[Gamma Persei|γ Persei]], [[Psi Persei|ψ Persei]], [[Delta Persei|δ Persei]], [[48 Persei]], [[Mu Persei|μ Persei]] and [[HD 27084]]. Consequently, α Persei itself is known as {{lang|zh|天船三}} ({{lang|zh-Latn|Tiān Chuán sān}}, {{lang-en|the Third Star of Celestial Boat}}.)<ref>{{zh icon}} [http://aeea.nmns.edu.tw/2006/0607/ap060711.html AEEA (Activities of Exhibition and Education in Astronomy) 天文教育資訊網 2006 年 7 月 11 日]</ref>
