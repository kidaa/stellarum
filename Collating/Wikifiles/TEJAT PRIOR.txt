'''Eta Geminorum''' (η Gem, η Geminorum) is a triple [[star system]] in the [[constellation]] [[Gemini (constellation)|Gemini]].  It has the traditional names '''Tejat Prior''', '''Propus''' and '''Praepes''' and '''Pish Pai''' (from the Persian Pīshpāy, پیش‌پای, meaning foreleg).<ref>p. 235, Star-names and Their Meanings, Richard Hinckley Allen, G. E. Stechert, 1899.</ref>

This star, along with [[Gamma Geminorum|γ Gem]] (Alhena), [[Mu Geminorum|μ Gem]] (Tejat Posterior), [[Nu Geminorum|ν Gem]] and [[Xi Geminorum|ξ Gem]] (Alzirr) were ''Al Han'ah'', "the brand" (on the neck of the camel). They also were associated in ''Al Nuḥātai'', the dual form of ''Al Nuḥāt'', "a Camel's Hump"<ref>{{cite book

In [[Chinese astronomy]], Tejat Prior is called 钺, [[Pinyin]]: Yuè, meaning ''	Battle Axe'', because this star is marking itself and stand alone in ''Battle Axe'' asterism, [[Well (Chinese constellation)|Well]] mansion (see : [[Chinese constellation]])<ref> {{zh icon}} [http://aeea.nmns.edu.tw/2006/0605/ap060526.html AEEA (Activities of Exhibition and Education in Astronomy) 天文教育資訊網 2006 年 5 月 26 日]</ref><ref>[http://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Gazetteer/Topics/astronomy/_Texts/secondary/ALLSTA/Gemini*.html Richard Hinckley Allen: Star Names — Their Lore and Meaning: Gemini]</ref>

==Properties==

Eta Geminorum A is a spectroscopic binary.  The primary is a [[semiregular variable star|semiregular]] [[variable star]] of type SRA whose brightness changes with a period of 234 days between [[apparent magnitude|magnitude]] +3.15 and +3.9. It is located 350 [[light year]]s from the [[Sun]] and is a [[red giant]] of the [[stellar classification|spectral type]] M3.  The secondary, probably a class B, orbits with a period of 8.2 years. Eta Geminorum B, a class G dwarf, orbits the pair with a period of at least 700 years.<ref>[http://adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/1944ApJ...100...63M The Spectrographic Orbit and Light-Variations of η Geminorum], Dean B. McLaughlin and Suzanne E. A. van Dijke, ''Astrophysical Journal'' '''100''' (July 1944), pp. 63&ndash;68.</ref><sup>,</sup><ref>[http://www.astro.uiuc.edu/~kaler/sow/propus.html Eta Geminorum] at Jim Kaler's STARS.</ref> 

Eta Geminorum is near the [[ecliptic]], so it can be [[occultation|occulted]] by the [[Moon]] and, very rarely, by [[planet]]s. The last occultation by a planet took place on July 27, 1910, by [[Venus]], and the next to last on July 11, 1837, by [[Mercury (planet)|Mercury]].

==Namesakes==
[[USS Propus (AK-132)]] was a [[United States Navy]] [[Crater class cargo ship]] named after the star.
