'''Delta Herculis''' (δ Her, δ Herculis) is a fourth-magnitude [[star]] in the [[constellation]] [[Hercules (constellation)|Hercules]]. It has the traditional name '''Sarin'''.<ref name="BBC1"/><ref name="WIKISKY"/>

== Properties ==

Sarin is a complex [[star system]] consisting of at least 2 stars and possibly as many as 5.  The main star is an A&nbsp;Type [[main sequence]] [[subgiant]] with a [[stellar classification]] A3IV.  The subgiant has both a mass and radius that are roughly two times solar yielding a total [[luminosity]] of about 18.5[[Solar luminosity|L<sub>☉</sub>]]<ref name="KALER"/>  Though it only shines with an [[apparent magnitude]] of 3.12, it the third brightest star in the [[Hercules (constellation)|Hercules]] constellation.  The most recent [[Hipparcos]] data yields a distance estimate of approximately 23.1 [[parsecs]] (pc) from Earth or 75 [[light year]]s (ly).

==In culture==
In [[Chinese language|Chinese]], {{lang|zh|天市左垣}} ({{lang|zh-Latn|Tiān Shì Zuǒ Yuán}}), meaning ''[[Heavenly Market enclosure|Left Wall of Heavenly Market Enclosure]]'', refers to an asterism which is represent eleven old states in China which is marking the left borderline of the enclosure, consisting of δ Herculis, [[Lambda Herculis|λ Herculis]], [[Mu Herculis|μ Herculis]], [[Omicron Herculis|ο Herculis]], [[112 Herculis]], [[Zeta Aquilae|ζ Aquilae]], [[Theta1 Serpentis|θ<sup>1</sup> Serpentis]], [[Eta Serpentis|η Serpentis]], [[Nu Ophiuchi|ν Ophiuchi]], [[Xi Serpentis|ξ Serpentis]] and [[Eta Ophiuchi|η Ophiuchi]].<ref>{{zh icon}} ''中國星座神話'', written by 陳久金. Published by 台灣書房出版有限公司, 2005, ISBN 978-986-7332-25-7.</ref> Consequently, δ Herculis itself is known as {{lang|zh|天市左垣一}} ({{lang|zh-Latn|Tiān Shì Zuǒ Yuán yī}}, {{lang-en|the First Star of Left Wall of Heavenly Market Enclosure}}), represent the state '''[[Wei (state)|Wei]]''' (魏),<ref>{{zh icon}} [http://www.lcsd.gov.hk/CE/Museum/Space/Research/StarName/c_research_chinengstars_c_d.htm 香港太空館 - 研究資源 - 亮星中英對照表], Hong Kong Space Museum. Accessed on line November 23, 2010.</ref><ref>{{zh icon}} [http://www.lcsd.gov.hk/CE/Museum/Space/Research/StarName/e_research_chinengstarzone_b.htm#HeavenlyMarketEnclosure English-Chinese Glossary of Chinese Star Regions, Asterisms and Star Name], Hong Kong Space Museum. Accessed on line November 23, 2010.</ref> together with [[33 Capricorni]] ([[Phi Capricorni|φ Capricorni]]  and [[Chi Capricorni|χ Capricorni]] in R.H. Allen's version)<ref>[http://penelope.uchicago.edu/Thayer/E/Gazetteer/Topics/astronomy/_Texts/secondary/ALLSTA/Capricornus*.html Star Names - R.H.Allen p.142]</ref> in ''[[Girl (Chinese constellation)|Twelve States]]'' (asterism).

In the catalogue of stars in the ''Calendarium'' of [[Al Achsasi al Mouakket]], this star was designated '''Menkib al Jathi al Aisr''', which was translated into [[Latin]] as '''Humerus Sinister Ingeniculi''', meaning ''the left shoulder of the kneeling man''.<ref name=mnras55_429/>
